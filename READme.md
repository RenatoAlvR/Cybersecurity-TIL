# General Cybersecurity Glossary
## Short description of various concepts

- **Cybersecurity:** The practice of ensuring confidentiality, integrity and availability of information by protecting networks, endpoints, people and data itself from unauthorized access or malicious attacks
- **Internal threat:** Threats that originate from within the organization, either intentional or unintentional.
- **External threat:** threat actors that originate from outside the organization, like a black hat hacker or rival companies with the intent of stealing information or sabotage systems.
- **PII:** Personally Identifiable Information, data that can be used to identify, contact, or locate a single person.
- **SPII:** Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information, PII that is protected by law or regulation, like social security numbers, credit card numbers, phone numbers, etc
- **HIPAA:** Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, U.S. legislation that protects sensitive patient health information.
- **GDPR:** General Data Protection Regulation, data protection and privacy law in the European Union and the European Economic Area.
- **Threat actor:** any person or organization that poses a security risk to an organization, system, or individual
- **SIEM:** Security Information and Event Management, software tools that collects and analyzes security events (like logs) from different parts and devices of an organization.
- **Social Engineering:** Psychological manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential information.
- **Phishing:** The use of digital communications to trick people or organizations to give up sensitive information digitally
- **Spear Phishing:** Targeted phishing attacks directed at specific individuals or organizations, often using personalized information to increase credibility
